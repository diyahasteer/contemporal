Clothing/Garments

Robe

CULTURE/PEOPLE: Oglala Lakota (Oglala Sioux)

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Painted

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments: Outerwear (flat)

PLACE: Pine Ridge, Pine Ridge Reservation; Oglala Lakota County; South Dakota; USA

COLLECTION HISTORY: Formerly owned by Chief Young-Man-Afraid-of-His-Horses; acquired from him in 1880 by John W. Alder (1844-1919), an Indian agent at Pine Ridge between 1879 and 1885; purchased by George Heye/MAI from John Alder or his son Eugene C. Alder (1876-1955) circa 1920.

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Sewn, ribbonwork appliqu√©, overlay beadwork

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments

Headdress with bone pin to secure it to a man's hair

CULTURE/PEOPLE: possibly South Appalachian Mississippian (archaeological culture) (attributed)

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Copper, animal bone

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments: Headwear and Headdresses

PLACE: Mound H, Burial 2; Moundville; Hale County and Tuscaloosa County; Alabama; USA

COLLECTION HISTORY: Excavated by archaeologist Clarence B. Moore (1852-1936) in 1905 or 1906; donated to the Academy of Natural Sciences of Philadelphia by C.B. Moore soon afterward; purchased by MAI from the Academy of Natural Sciences with other C.B. Moore collection materials in 1929.

Chief's headdress representing a killer whale with a raven on his back

CULTURE/PEOPLE: Kwakwaka'wakw (Kwakiutl)

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Red cedar, yellow cedar, pigment/pigments, velveteen

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments: Headwear and Headdresses

PLACE: Quatsino; Mount Waddington Regional District; British Columbia; Canada

COLLECTION HISTORY: Formerly owned and used by Chief Willie Seaweed (Kwakwaka'wakw, 1873-1967); purchased from him by Wilhelm Helmer; purchased by MAI from Wilhelm Helmer in 1966.

Headdress

CULTURE/PEOPLE: probably Plains (attributed)

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Felt, chicken feather/feathers, turkey feather/feathers, glass bead/beads, rabbit skin/fur, synthetic ribbon, glue, adhesive tape, hide thong/babiche, thread, horsehair

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments

PLACE: Plains; USA, Canada (inferred)

COLLECTION HISTORY: Formerly in the collection of Senator Daniel K. Inouye (1924-2012, D-Hawaii) and probably received as a gift in recognition of his service to American Indians as Chairman of the Senate Committee on Indian Affairs (1987-1995 and 2001-2003); donated to NMAI by Senator Inouye's widow Irene Hirano Inouye (1948-2020) in 2013.

Indigenous Women Rise

CULTURE/PEOPLE: Apsáalooke (Crow/Absaroke)

MEDIA/MATERIALS: Cotton cloth, ink

OBJECT TYPE: Clothing/Garments: Accessories

PLACE: USA (inferred)

COLLECTION HISTORY: Commissioned by Native Americans in Philanthropy (Minneapolis, Minnesota, and Los Angeles) for "Indigenous Women Rise" participants at the January 21, 2017 Women’s March in Washington, DC; presented to NMAI Director Kevin Gover (Pawnee) by an unknown party after the March; donated to NMAI by Kevin Gover in 2017.
